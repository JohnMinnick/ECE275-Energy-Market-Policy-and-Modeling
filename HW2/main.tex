\section{Least-Cost Dispatch with Transmission Constraints}

\subsection{Problem Setup}
\textit{Prompt: Consider a 3-node triangle network (A, B, C) with 200 MW demand at each node. 
Node C has 500 MW capacity at \$26/MWh; Node B has 250 MW capacity at \$22/MWh. 
Reactances: A-B = 6.0, B-C = 6.0, A-C = 4.8. 
Link A-B has a flow limit of 45 MW in each direction.}\\

The system has a total demand of 600 MW across three nodes, served by two generators. The cheapest generator (B, \$22/MWh) has limited capacity (250 MW), so the more expensive generator C must supply the remainder. The network topology creates a triangle with a single independent voltage loop, and the A-B link is constrained.

\subsection{Part A: LP Formulation and Constrained Dispatch}

The LP uses explicit flow variables with Kirchhoff's laws:

\textbf{Decision Variables:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item $g_B, g_C$ = generation at nodes B and C (MW)
    \item $t_{mn}, t_{nm}$ = positive flow components on each link (6 variables)
    \item Net flow from $m \to n = t_{mn} - t_{nm}$
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Objective:} Minimize $22 g_B + 26 g_C$

\textbf{Constraints:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{KCL at Node A:} $(t_{BA} - t_{AB}) + (t_{CA} - t_{AC}) = 200$
    \item \textbf{KCL at Node B:} $g_B + (t_{AB} - t_{BA}) + (t_{CB} - t_{BC}) = 200$
    \item \textbf{KCL at Node C:} $g_C + (t_{AC} - t_{CA}) + (t_{BC} - t_{CB}) = 200$
    \item \textbf{KVL (loop $A \to B \to C \to A$):} $6.0(t_{AB} - t_{BA}) + 6.0(t_{BC} - t_{CB}) - 4.8(t_{AC} - t_{CA}) = 0$
    \item \textbf{Flow limit:} $t_{AB} \leq 45$, $t_{BA} \leq 45$
    \item \textbf{Capacity:} $0 \leq g_B \leq 250$, $0 \leq g_C \leq 500$
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Constrained Solution:}
\begin{table}[h]
    \centering
    \caption{Constrained Dispatch Results (A-B limit = 45 MW)}
    \label{tab:constrained}
    \begin{tabular}{@{}lrr@{}}
    \toprule
    \textbf{Variable} & \textbf{Value} & \textbf{Unit} \\ \midrule
    Total Cost & \$14,936.00 & \$ \\
    Gen B & 166.0 & MW \\
    Gen C & 434.0 & MW \\
    Flow A$\to$B (net) & $-45.0$ & MW \\
    Flow B$\to$C (net) & $-79.0$ & MW \\
    Flow A$\to$C (net) & $-155.0$ & MW \\
    \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{table}

The negative flows indicate that power flows \textit{towards} node A from both generators. The A-B link is at its 45 MW limit (binding), with 45 MW flowing from B to A. The majority of power (155 MW) flows from C to A via the direct A-C link, and 79 MW flows from C to B (some of which B then forwards to A).

\subsection{Part B: Locational Marginal Prices (LMPs)}

The dual variables on the KCL constraints give the LMP at each node:

\begin{table}[h]
    \centering
    \caption{Locational Marginal Prices}
    \label{tab:lmps}
    \begin{tabular}{@{}lr@{}}
    \toprule
    \textbf{Node} & \textbf{LMP (\$/MWh)} \\ \midrule
    A & \cellcolor{myred} \$29.20 \\
    B & \cellcolor{mygreen} \$22.00 \\
    C & \cellcolor{myyellow} \$26.00 \\
    \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{table}

These were verified by incrementing load by 1 MW at each node and observing the change in total cost, which matched the dual variables exactly.

\textbf{Interpretation:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Node B (\$22/MWh):} An extra 1 MW of demand at B is met by increasing Gen B by 1 MW at its marginal cost of \$22/MWh. Gen B still has spare capacity (166 MW out of 250 MW).
    \item \textbf{Node C (\$26/MWh):} An extra 1 MW of demand at C is met by increasing Gen C by 1 MW at its marginal cost of \$26/MWh. Gen C also has spare capacity.
    \item \textbf{Node A (\$29.20/MWh):} This is the ``odd'' result. Node A's LMP is \textit{higher than both generators}. This occurs because A has no local generator and is served entirely through transmission. With the A-B link congested at 45 MW, a 1 MW increase at A cannot simply be served by the cheap Gen B. Instead, due to the KVL constraint and loop flows, the system must use a more expensive combination of adjustments: increasing Gen C while decreasing Gen B, pushing more power through the unconstrained A-C path. This ``re-dispatch'' cost exceeds either generator's individual marginal cost.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Part C: Cost of Congestion}

\begin{table}[h]
    \centering
    \caption{Cost of Congestion}
    \label{tab:congestion_cost}
    \begin{tabular}{@{}lr@{}}
    \toprule
    \textbf{Scenario} & \textbf{Total Cost} \\ \midrule
    Constrained (45 MW limit) & \$14,936.00 \\
    Unconstrained (no limit) & \$14,600.00 \\
    \textbf{Cost of Congestion} & \cellcolor{myred} \textbf{\$336.00} \\
    \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{table}

Without the transmission constraint, the optimizer fully utilizes Gen B (250 MW at \$22) before dispatching Gen C (350 MW at \$26), saving \$336. The constraint becomes non-binding at an A-B flow limit of \textbf{75 MW}. At or above that level, the unconstrained solution's natural flow of 75 MW on A-B is feasible.

\subsection{Part D: Congestion Surplus}

The ``congestion surplus'' (ISO net revenue) is the difference between what consumers pay and what generators receive, when both are priced at their local LMP.

\textbf{Consumer Payments:}
\begin{align*}
    \text{Consumers} &= \text{LMP}_A \times D_A + \text{LMP}_B \times D_B + \text{LMP}_C \times D_C \\
    &= \$29.20 \times 200 + \$22.00 \times 200 + \$26.00 \times 200 \\
    &= \$15{,}440.00
\end{align*}

\textbf{Generator Revenue:}
\begin{align*}
    \text{Generators} &= \text{LMP}_B \times g_B + \text{LMP}_C \times g_C \\
    &= \$22.00 \times 166 + \$26.00 \times 434 \\
    &= \$14{,}936.00
\end{align*}

\textbf{Congestion Surplus:}
$$\text{Congestion Surplus} = \$15{,}440 - \$14{,}936 = \$504.00$$

The congestion surplus (\$504) is \textbf{not equal} to the cost of congestion (\$336). The cost of congestion measures the \textit{production cost increase} due to the constraint, while the congestion surplus measures the \textit{price differential revenue} collected by the ISO. These are fundamentally different quantities: the surplus depends on the spread between nodal LMPs and total demand, while the cost of congestion depends only on the generation re-dispatch cost.

\section{Power Transmission Distribution Factors (PTDFs)}

\subsection{Problem Setup}
\textit{Prompt: Same 3-node triangle network. Reactances: A-B = 1.7, B-C = 1.7, A-C = 1.4. Hub node is A. Calculate PTDFs for injections at various nodes.}\\

\subsection{Methodology}

The PTDF calculation follows these steps:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Compute susceptances:} $b_{mn} = 1/R_{mn}$
    \item \textbf{Build the nodal susceptance matrix} $\mathbf{B}$ (3$\times$3)
    \item \textbf{Remove the hub node} (A) to get $\mathbf{B}_{\text{red}}$ (2$\times$2)
    \item \textbf{Invert:} $\mathbf{X} = \mathbf{B}_{\text{red}}^{-1}$
    \item \textbf{Calculate:} $\text{PTDF}_{m, j \to k} = b_{jk} \cdot (X_{mj} - X_{mk})$
\end{enumerate}

Where $X_{A,*} = 0$ by convention (hub node).

\subsection{Susceptance Matrix}

$$b_{AB} = b_{BC} = \frac{1}{1.7} = 0.5882, \quad b_{AC} = \frac{1}{1.4} = 0.7143$$

$$\mathbf{B}_{\text{red}} = \begin{pmatrix} b_{AB} + b_{BC} & -b_{BC} \\ -b_{BC} & b_{BC} + b_{AC} \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1.1765 & -0.5882 \\ -0.5882 & 1.3025 \end{pmatrix}$$

$$\mathbf{X} = \mathbf{B}_{\text{red}}^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} 1.0979 & 0.4958 \\ 0.4958 & 0.9917 \end{pmatrix}$$

Where rows/columns correspond to nodes B (index 0) and C (index 1).

\subsection{PTDF Results}

\begin{table}[h]
    \centering
    \caption{PTDF Calculations (Hub = A)}
    \label{tab:ptdfs}
    \begin{tabular}{@{}lcl@{}}
    \toprule
    \textbf{PTDF} & \textbf{Value} & \textbf{Calculation} \\ \midrule
    $\text{PTDF}_{C,AB}$ & $-0.2917$ & $b_{AB}(X_{CA} - X_{CB}) = 0.5882(0 - 0.4958)$ \\
    $\text{PTDF}_{C,BA}$ & $+0.2917$ & $b_{BA}(X_{CB} - X_{CA}) = 0.5882(0.4958 - 0)$ \\
    $\text{PTDF}_{C,AC}$ & $-0.7083$ & $b_{AC}(X_{CA} - X_{CC}) = 0.7143(0 - 0.9917)$ \\
    $\text{PTDF}_{C,BC}$ & $-0.2917$ & $b_{BC}(X_{CB} - X_{CC}) = 0.5882(0.4958 - 0.9917)$ \\
    $\text{PTDF}_{B,AC}$ & $-0.3542$ & $b_{AC}(X_{BA} - X_{BC}) = 0.7143(0 - 0.4958)$ \\
    $\text{PTDF}_{A,AC}$ & $0.0000$ & $b_{AC}(X_{AA} - X_{AC}) = 0.7143(0 - 0)$ \\
    \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{table}

\subsection{Interpretation}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{$\text{PTDF}_{C,AB} = -0.2917$:} A 1 MW injection at C (withdrawal at hub A) induces 0.2917 MW flowing from B to A (negative means flow is opposite to the A$\to$B direction). The flow splits: about 70.8\% goes directly from C to A, and 29.2\% takes the indirect path C$\to$B$\to$A.
    
    \item \textbf{$\text{PTDF}_{C,BA} = +0.2917$:} This is simply $-\text{PTDF}_{C,AB}$, confirming that reversing the direction of a line negates its PTDF.
    
    \item \textbf{$\text{PTDF}_{C,AC} = -0.7083$:} The majority of the injected power at C flows directly to A on the A-C link (70.83\%). The negative sign indicates flow from C to A.
    
    \item \textbf{$\text{PTDF}_{C,BC} = -0.2917$:} 29.17\% of the injection flows from C to B. Note that $|\text{PTDF}_{C,AC}| + |\text{PTDF}_{C,BC}| = 1.0$, confirming that all power leaving C is accounted for.
    
    \item \textbf{$\text{PTDF}_{B,AC} = -0.3542$:} A 1 MW injection at B (withdrawal at A) induces 0.3542 MW on the A-C link (flowing from C toward A).
    
    \item \textbf{$\text{PTDF}_{A,AC} = 0$:} An injection at the hub node A with withdrawal at hub A means zero net injection, hence zero induced flow on any line.
\end{itemize}

"""
ECE275 Homework 2: Linearized DC Load Flow
Solves: (1) Least-Cost Dispatch with Transmission Constraints
        (2) PTDF Calculation
"""
import numpy as np
from scipy.optimize import linprog
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

def solve_dispatch():
    """Problem 1: LP-based dispatch with KCL/KVL constraints."""
    # Demand (MW) and generator data
    D_A, D_B, D_C = 200.0, 200.0, 200.0
    Cap_B, MC_B = 250.0, 22.0
    Cap_C, MC_C = 500.0, 26.0
    R_AB, R_BC, R_AC = 6.0, 6.0, 4.8
    F_AB_max = 45.0

    # Decision vars: [g_B, g_C, t_AB, t_BA, t_BC, t_CB, t_AC, t_CA]
    c = np.array([MC_B, MC_C, 0, 0, 0, 0, 0, 0])

    A_eq = np.array([
        [0, 0, -1, 1, 0, 0, -1, 1],        # KCL Node A
        [1, 0,  1,-1,-1, 1,  0, 0],         # KCL Node B
        [0, 1,  0, 0, 1,-1,  1,-1],         # KCL Node C
        [0, 0, R_AB,-R_AB, R_BC,-R_BC, -R_AC, R_AC],  # KVL
    ], dtype=float)
    b_eq = np.array([D_A, D_B, D_C, 0.0])

    big_M = 10000.0
    bounds_con = [(0,Cap_B),(0,Cap_C),(0,F_AB_max),(0,F_AB_max),
                  (0,big_M),(0,big_M),(0,big_M),(0,big_M)]
    bounds_uncon = [(0,Cap_B),(0,Cap_C),(0,big_M),(0,big_M),
                    (0,big_M),(0,big_M),(0,big_M),(0,big_M)]

    res_con = linprog(c, A_eq=A_eq, b_eq=b_eq, bounds=bounds_con, method='highs')
    res_uncon = linprog(c, A_eq=A_eq, b_eq=b_eq, bounds=bounds_uncon, method='highs')

    # LMPs from dual variables
    duals = res_con.eqlin.marginals
    LMP_A, LMP_B, LMP_C = duals[0], duals[1], duals[2]

    # Congestion surplus
    x = res_con.x
    consumer_pay = LMP_A*D_A + LMP_B*D_B + LMP_C*D_C
    gen_rev = LMP_B*x[0] + LMP_C*x[1]
    surplus = consumer_pay - gen_rev

    print(f"Constrained: Cost=${res_con.fun:,.2f}, B={x[0]:.0f}MW, C={x[1]:.0f}MW")
    print(f"Unconstrained: Cost=${res_uncon.fun:,.2f}")
    print(f"LMPs: A=${LMP_A:.2f}, B=${LMP_B:.2f}, C=${LMP_C:.2f}")
    print(f"Congestion cost: ${res_con.fun - res_uncon.fun:,.2f}")
    print(f"Congestion surplus: ${surplus:,.2f}")

def solve_ptdf():
    """Problem 2: PTDF via susceptance matrix inversion."""
    b_AB = 1/1.7; b_BC = 1/1.7; b_AC = 1/1.4
    B_red = np.array([[b_AB+b_BC, -b_BC], [-b_BC, b_BC+b_AC]])
    X = np.linalg.inv(B_red)  # Rows/cols: B=0, C=1; A=hub (all zeros)

    def ptdf(m, j, k):
        idx = {'B':0, 'C':1}
        b = {'AB':b_AB,'BA':b_AB,'BC':b_BC,'CB':b_BC,'AC':b_AC,'CA':b_AC}
        X_mj = X[idx[m], idx[j]] if m!='A' and j!='A' else 0
        X_mk = X[idx[m], idx[k]] if m!='A' and k!='A' else 0
        return b[j+k] * (X_mj - X_mk)

    for name, args in [('C,AB',('C','A','B')), ('C,BA',('C','B','A')),
                        ('C,AC',('C','A','C')), ('C,BC',('C','B','C')),
                        ('B,AC',('B','A','C')), ('A,AC',('A','A','C'))]:
        print(f"PTDF_{{{name}}} = {ptdf(*args):.6f}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    solve_dispatch()
    solve_ptdf()
